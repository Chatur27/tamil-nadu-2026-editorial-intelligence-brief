# 163 Seats Changed Hands: Tamil Nadu 2026 Editorial Intelligence Brief

An editorial intelligence brief for **AtliQ Media** explaining Tamil Nadu's 2026 Assembly election results through **scale, geography, and competitiveness** using official result data.

## Core headline

**163 of 234 constituencies changed winning party between 2021 and 2026.**

Most election analysis starts with who won. This project starts with what changed, then turns the analysis into a neutral one-hour show structure for AtliQ Media.

## AtliQ 3-Signal Editorial Framework

1. **Scale** — How much changed?
   - 163 of 234 constituencies changed winning party.
2. **Geography** — Where did the map change most?
   - Chennai Metro: 30 of 32 seats changed winning party.
3. **Competitiveness** — How close were the wins?
   - 103 constituencies were won by less than 5 percentage points.

## Recommended one-hour show structure

| Segment | Runtime | Focus |
|---|---:|---|
| Opening | 0–15 min | Scale of change: 163 seats changed hands |
| Chapter 1 | 15–35 min | Geography of change: regional shifts, especially Chennai Metro |
| Chapter 2 | 35–55 min | Competitiveness: narrower margins and close contests |

## Methodology summary

- The analysis compares the winning party in each Assembly Constituency between 2021 and 2026.
- A constituency is classified as “changed” when the winning party in 2026 differs from the winning party in 2021.
- Victory margin is calculated as winner votes minus runner-up votes.
- Margin percentage is calculated using the constituency vote total represented in the dataset.
- The analysis avoids causal claims, voter-transfer claims, and predictions.

## Data and limitations

The project uses the Codebasics starter-pack election datasets and official result-data context. The 2026 starter-pack data was prepared from the ECI live results portal. Before real broadcast use, figures should be checked against final AC-level Form 20 data where available.

This analysis can describe:
- who won each constituency;
- which constituencies changed winning party;
- regional patterns in the winner map;
- victory margins and close contests.

This analysis cannot prove:
- why voters made choices;
- that individual voters moved from one party to another;
- causal explanations for gains/losses;
- future election outcomes.

## Positioning

This is not a political recap. It is a stakeholder-ready, neutral, data-backed editorial brief designed to help a newsroom explain a complex result clearly and responsibly.
